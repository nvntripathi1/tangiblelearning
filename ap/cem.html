Course Overview

Our AP Physics C: Electricity and Magnetism course represents the pinnacle of high school electromagnetic theory education. This advanced course provides a rigorous, calculus-based treatment of electric and magnetic phenomena, preparing students for the most challenging concepts in physics and engineering.

AP Physics C: E&M is equivalent to a semester-long, calculus-based college electromagnetism course. Students will master Maxwell's equations, electromagnetic induction, and advanced circuit analysis using sophisticated mathematical tools including vector calculus, differential equations, and complex mathematical modeling.

Detailed Course Curriculum

  • Unit 1: Electrostatics 10 hours

    Advanced treatment of electric fields, potential, and Gauss's law with calculus.

    • Coulomb's law and electric field calculations using integration
    • Gauss's law and its applications to complex geometries
    • Electric potential and potential energy with calculus
    • Conductors and electrostatic equilibrium
    • Dielectrics and boundary conditions

  • Unit 2: Conductors, Capacitors, and Dielectrics 8 hours

    Advanced capacitance theory and electrostatic energy storage.

    • Capacitance calculations using integration and field theory
    • Energy storage in electric fields
    • Dielectric materials and their electromagnetic properties
    • Complex capacitor networks and energy analysis
    • Electrostatic stress and force calculations

  • Unit 3: Electric Circuits 8 hours

    Advanced circuit analysis including transient behavior and AC circuits.

    • Kirchhoff's laws with calculus-based analysis
    • RC and LC circuits: transient analysis using differential equations
    • AC circuit analysis with complex impedance
    • Resonance in RLC circuits
    • Power analysis in AC circuits

  • Unit 4: Magnetic Fields 9 hours

    Comprehensive treatment of magnetostatics using vector calculus.

    • Magnetic fields of moving charges and current distributions
    • Biot-Savart law and its applications
    • Amp√®re's law and its use in complex geometries
    • Magnetic forces on charges and current-carrying conductors
    • Magnetic dipoles and their interactions

  • Unit 5: Electromagnetic Induction 10 hours

    Faraday's law, Lenz's law, and applications of electromagnetic induction.

    • Faraday's law with advanced mathematical treatment
    • Motional EMF and its calculus-based derivation
    • Self-inductance and mutual inductance calculations
    • Energy storage in magnetic fields
    • Electromagnetic induction in complex geometries

  • Unit 6: Electromagnetic Waves 6 hours

    Maxwell's equations and electromagnetic wave propagation.

    • Maxwell's equations in integral and differential form
    • Electromagnetic wave derivation from Maxwell's equations
    • Wave propagation and energy transport
    • Electromagnetic spectrum and wave properties
    • Boundary conditions and wave reflection/transmission

  • Unit 7: Review and Advanced Applications 4 hours

    Comprehensive review and preparation for the AP Physics C: E&M exam.

    • Integration of all electromagnetic concepts
    • Advanced problem-solving strategies
    • Practice with released AP exam questions
    • Mathematical techniques review
    • Final exam preparation and confidence building
