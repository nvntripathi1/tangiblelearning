Course Overview

Our AP Physics 2 course is designed to build upon the foundations established in AP Physics 1, diving deeper into advanced algebra-based physics concepts. This comprehensive program covers the remaining four Big Ideas from the College Board curriculum, including fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, electricity and magnetism, waves and optics, and quantum, atomic, and nuclear physics.

AP Physics 2 is equivalent to a second-semester college course in algebra-based physics. The course emphasizes conceptual understanding, mathematical reasoning, and the application of physics principles to solve complex, real-world problems. Students will develop advanced analytical skills and gain exposure to modern physics concepts that form the foundation for further study in physics, engineering, and related fields.

Important Prerequisites

Required: Successful completion of AP Physics 1 or equivalent first-year physics course

Mathematics: Strong foundation in algebra, trigonometry, and basic logarithms

Recommended: Some exposure to exponential functions and basic calculus concepts (though not required)

AP Exam Information

Exam Date: May 2026 | Duration: 3 hours | Format: Multiple Choice (50%) + Free Response (50%)

The exam tests deep conceptual understanding and the ability to apply physics principles across multiple contexts, with emphasis on mathematical reasoning and laboratory skills.

Detailed Course Curriculum

  • Unit 1: Fluids 8 hours

    Comprehensive study of fluid statics and dynamics, including pressure, buoyancy, and fluid flow.

    • Fluid pressure and Pascal's principle
    • Buoyancy and Archimedes' principle
    • Fluid dynamics and Bernoulli's equation
    • Viscosity and laminar vs. turbulent flow
    • Applications to real-world fluid systems

  • Unit 2: Thermodynamics 10 hours

    Heat, temperature, and the laws of thermodynamics with applications to various thermal processes.

    • Temperature, heat, and thermal equilibrium
    • Specific heat capacity and phase changes
    • Ideal gas law and kinetic theory
    • First and second laws of thermodynamics
    • Heat engines, refrigerators, and entropy

  • Unit 3: Electric Force, Field, and Potential 9 hours

    Advanced electrostatics including electric field, potential, and capacitance.

    • Coulomb's law and electric field calculations
    • Electric potential and potential difference
    • Capacitance and energy storage in capacitors
    • Dielectrics and their effects on capacitance
    • Electric field and potential in various geometries

  • Unit 4: Electric Circuits 8 hours

    Advanced circuit analysis including RC circuits and complex network analysis.

    • Kirchhoff's laws and circuit analysis techniques
    • RC circuits: charging and discharging
    • Complex resistor-capacitor networks
    • Electrical power and energy in circuits
    • Measuring instruments and circuit applications

  • Unit 5: Magnetism and Electromagnetic Induction 10 hours

    Magnetic fields, forces, and electromagnetic induction phenomena.

    • Magnetic fields and forces on moving charges
    • Magnetic forces on current-carrying conductors
    • Sources of magnetic fields: straight wires, loops, solenoids
    • Faraday's law and electromagnetic induction
    • Lenz's law and motional EMF

  • Unit 6: Geometric and Physical Optics 8 hours

    Light behavior including reflection, refraction, interference, and diffraction.

    • Reflection and refraction of light
    • Total internal reflection and optical fibers
    • Lens and mirror optics
    • Wave interference and Young's double-slit experiment
    • Diffraction gratings and polarization

  • Unit 7: Quantum, Atomic, and Nuclear Physics 8 hours

    Introduction to modern physics concepts including quantum mechanics and nuclear physics.

    • Photons and the photoelectric effect
    • Energy levels and electron transitions in atoms
    • Wave-particle duality and de Broglie wavelength
    • Nuclear structure and radioactive decay
    • Mass-energy equivalence and nuclear reactions

  • Unit 8: Review and Exam Preparation 4 hours

    Comprehensive review, exam strategies, and final preparation for the AP Physics 2 exam.

    • Comprehensive review of all major concepts
    • Practice with released AP exam questions
    • Time management and exam strategy techniques
    • Final assessment and performance analysis
    • Last-minute clarifications and confidence building
