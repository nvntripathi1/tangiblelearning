Course Overview

Our AP Precalculus course is designed to provide students with a comprehensive foundation in advanced mathematical concepts essential for success in calculus and higher-level mathematics. This course bridges the gap between Algebra 2 and AP Calculus, emphasizing deep conceptual understanding, mathematical reasoning, and problem-solving techniques.

AP Precalculus covers polynomial, rational, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions, along with their applications. Students will develop strong analytical skills through the study of sequences, series, and mathematical modeling. This course prepares students not only for the AP exam but also for success in AP Calculus and college-level mathematics.

Detailed Course Curriculum

  • Unit 1: Polynomial and Rational Functions 12 hours

    Comprehensive study of polynomial and rational functions, their properties, and applications.

    • Polynomial functions: degree, leading coefficient, and end behavior
    • Zeros, factors, and the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra
    • Rational functions: asymptotes, discontinuities, and behavior
    • Function transformations and their effects on graphs
    • Applications and mathematical modeling with polynomial and rational functions

  • Unit 2: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions 10 hours

    Exponential and logarithmic functions with applications to growth, decay, and modeling.

    • Exponential functions: properties, graphs, and applications
    • Logarithmic functions and their relationship to exponentials
    • Properties of logarithms and solving exponential equations
    • Applications: compound interest, population growth, radioactive decay
    • Mathematical modeling with exponential and logarithmic functions

  • Unit 3: Trigonometric and Polar Functions 15 hours

    Comprehensive trigonometry including functions, identities, and polar coordinates.

    • Unit circle and trigonometric functions
    • Graphs of trigonometric functions and their transformations
    • Trigonometric identities and solving trigonometric equations
    • Applications of trigonometry to real-world problems
    • Introduction to polar coordinates and polar functions

  • Unit 4: Functions Involving Parameters, Vectors, and Matrices 10 hours

    Advanced function concepts including parametric equations, vectors, and matrices.

    • Parametric equations and their applications
    • Vector operations and applications
    • Matrix operations and systems of equations
    • Function composition and inverse functions
    • Advanced function analysis and transformations

  • Unit 5: Sequences and Series 8 hours

    Arithmetic and geometric sequences and series with applications.

    • Arithmetic sequences and series
    • Geometric sequences and series
    • Convergence and divergence of infinite series
    • Applications of sequences and series
    • Mathematical induction and recursive relationships

  • Unit 6: Exam Preparation and Review 5 hours

    Comprehensive review and exam preparation strategies.

    • Review of all major concepts and problem-solving strategies
    • Practice with released AP Precalculus exam questions
    • Time management and exam strategy techniques
    • Final assessment and performance analysis
    • Last-minute review and confidence building
