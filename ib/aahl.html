Course Overview

Our IB Mathematics AA HL course is designed for students with a strong interest in mathematics and those planning to pursue mathematics, engineering, physical sciences, or economics at university. This comprehensive year-long course covers advanced mathematical topics with rigorous analytical approaches, emphasizing algebraic manipulation, proof techniques, and sophisticated problem-solving skills.

IB Mathematics AA HL is the most demanding mathematics course in the IB programme, equivalent to first-year university mathematics. It covers calculus, algebra, functions, trigonometry, statistics, and probability at an advanced level, developing both computational fluency and deep conceptual understanding essential for success in STEM fields.

Higher Level Advantages

Mathematics AA HL provides intensive mathematical training, advanced problem-solving techniques, and preparation for university-level mathematics. HL students develop the analytical skills highly valued by top universities in mathematics, engineering, and sciences.

HL assessments require advanced algebraic manipulation, rigorous proof techniques, and sophisticated mathematical reasoning across all topics.

Detailed Course Curriculum

  • Topic 1: Number and Algebra 39 hours

    Advanced algebraic concepts, sequences, series, and mathematical induction.

    • Complex number operations and De Moivre's theorem
    • Advanced sequences and series including convergence tests
    • Mathematical induction and proof techniques
    • Binomial theorem and its applications
    • Partial fractions and advanced algebraic manipulation
    • Polynomial theory and factor/remainder theorems

  • Topic 2: Functions and Equations 24 hours

    Advanced function analysis, transformations, and equation solving.

    • Domain, range, and advanced function composition
    • Function transformations and their graphical interpretations
    • Inverse functions and their properties
    • Rational functions and asymptotic behavior
    • Advanced equation solving techniques
    • Systems of equations and inequalities

  • Topic 3: Circular Functions and Trigonometry 16 hours

    Advanced trigonometric concepts, identities, and applications.

    • Unit circle and radian measures
    • Advanced trigonometric identities and equations
    • Sum and product formulas
    • Inverse trigonometric functions
    • Complex trigonometric applications
    • Harmonic functions and modeling

  • Topic 4: Vectors 16 hours

    Advanced vector operations, geometry, and applications.

    • Vector operations in 2D and 3D space
    • Scalar and vector products
    • Vector equations of lines and planes
    • Angle calculations and vector projections
    • Applications in geometry and physics
    • Vector spaces and linear independence

  • Topic 5: Statistics and Probability 16 hours

    Advanced statistical methods and probability distributions.

    • Descriptive statistics and data analysis
    • Probability theory and conditional probability
    • Discrete and continuous probability distributions
    • Binomial, normal, and Poisson distributions
    • Hypothesis testing and confidence intervals
    • Correlation and regression analysis

  • Topic 6: Calculus HL Extension 48 hours

    Advanced differential and integral calculus with applications.

    • Limits, continuity, and advanced differentiation
    • Applications of derivatives: optimization, related rates
    • Advanced integration techniques and applications
    • Differential equations and their solutions
    • Maclaurin and Taylor series expansions
    • Multivariable calculus concepts
    • Area, volume, and advanced applications

  • Mathematical Exploration Preparation

    Comprehensive support for your individual mathematical investigation.

    • Research question development and mathematical focus
    • Advanced mathematical investigation techniques
    • Proper use of mathematical notation and terminology
    • Critical reflection and evaluation of results
    • Professional mathematical communication and presentation
