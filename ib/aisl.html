Course Overview

Our IB Mathematics AI SL course is designed for students who enjoy mathematics and want to see how it applies to the real world. This comprehensive year-long course emphasizes practical applications, technology integration, and statistical reasoning. Mathematics AI SL is perfect for students planning careers in social sciences, business, psychology, or any field where data analysis and mathematical modeling are important.

IB Mathematics AI SL focuses on applications and interpretation of mathematics, using technology to solve real-world problems. The course covers statistics, probability, modeling, geometry, and basic calculus concepts, always with an emphasis on practical understanding and technological proficiency rather than abstract mathematical theory.

Standard Level Focus

Mathematics AI SL provides essential mathematical skills for university study and careers in business, social sciences, and applied fields. The course emphasizes understanding, application, and technology use rather than complex theoretical proofs.

Detailed Course Curriculum

  • Topic 1: Number and Algebra 19 hours

    Practical number systems, algebraic manipulation, and equation solving.

    • Number systems and significant figures
    • Practical algebra and formula manipulation
    • Linear and quadratic equations with applications
    • Exponential and logarithmic functions
    • Arithmetic and geometric sequences in context
    • Financial mathematics and compound interest

  • Topic 2: Functions 31 hours

    Function concepts with emphasis on modeling and applications.

    • Function notation and practical applications
    • Linear functions and modeling
    • Quadratic functions and optimization
    • Exponential and logarithmic modeling
    • Trigonometric functions and periodic phenomena
    • Function transformations and graphing technology

  • Topic 3: Geometry and Trigonometry 25 hours

    Practical geometry and trigonometry for real-world applications.

    • 2D and 3D geometry with practical applications
    • Right triangle trigonometry
    • Sine and cosine rules for any triangle
    • Area and volume calculations
    • Coordinate geometry and applications
    • Using technology for geometric problem-solving

  • Topic 4: Statistics and Probability 36 hours

    Comprehensive data analysis and probability with real-world focus.

    • Descriptive statistics and data presentation
    • Correlation and regression analysis
    • Probability theory and practical applications
    • Discrete and continuous probability distributions
    • Normal distribution and its applications
    • Statistical inference and hypothesis testing
    • Using technology for statistical analysis

  • Topic 5: Calculus 14 hours

    Introduction to calculus concepts with practical applications.

    • Limits and continuity concepts
    • Derivative as rate of change
    • Differentiation rules and applications
    • Optimization problems in context
    • Integration as area and accumulation
    • Using technology for calculus calculations

  • Mathematical Exploration Preparation

    Comprehensive support for your individual mathematical investigation.

    • Research question development with practical focus
    • Data collection and analysis techniques
    • Appropriate use of technology and software
    • Mathematical modeling and interpretation
    • Clear communication and professional presentation
