Course Overview

Our IB Physics SL course is designed to develop your understanding of fundamental physics principles while fostering scientific inquiry skills essential for the IB Diploma Programme. This comprehensive year-long course covers all core topics of the IB Physics SL curriculum, emphasizing conceptual understanding, mathematical modeling, and practical investigation skills.

IB Physics SL provides a solid foundation in classical and modern physics, preparing you for higher education in science, engineering, and technology. The course emphasizes the interconnected nature of physics concepts and their applications in real-world contexts, developing both your analytical thinking and communication skills.

IB Assessment Information

External Assessment (80%): Paper 1 (Multiple Choice - 45 min) + Paper 2 (Structured Questions - 75 min) + Paper 3 (Data Analysis - 60 min)

Internal Assessment (20%): Individual Investigation (10 hours) - Design, conduct, and analyze your own physics experiment

Exams emphasize data analysis, scientific reasoning, and application of physics principles to novel situations.

Internal Assessment (IA) Support

Comprehensive guidance for your Individual Investigation including topic selection, experimental design, data collection and analysis, evaluation, and presentation. We'll help you achieve excellence in this crucial 20% component of your final grade.

Detailed Course Curriculum

  • Topic 1: Measurements and Uncertainties 8 hours

    Fundamental measurement concepts, uncertainty analysis, and data presentation.

    • Measurement in physics and SI units
    • Uncertainties and errors in measurement
    • Vectors and scalars
    • Significant figures and scientific notation
    • Graphical techniques and data analysis

  • Topic 2: Mechanics 22 hours

    Motion, forces, work, energy, and momentum in mechanical systems.

    • Motion - displacement, velocity, acceleration
    • Forces and Newton's laws of motion
    • Work, energy, and power
    • Momentum and impulse
    • Uniform circular motion and gravitation

  • Topic 3: Thermal Physics 11 hours

    Temperature, heat, and the behavior of matter at different scales.

    • Thermal concepts and temperature scales
    • Modeling gases - kinetic model and ideal gas law
    • First law of thermodynamics
    • Specific heat capacity and latent heat
    • Heat transfer mechanisms

  • Topic 4: Waves 15 hours

    Oscillations, wave properties, and wave behavior phenomena.

    • Simple harmonic motion
    • Traveling waves - wave equation and characteristics
    • Wave characteristics and properties
    • Wave behavior - reflection, refraction, diffraction
    • Standing waves and resonance

  • Topic 5: Electricity and Magnetism 15 hours

    Electric fields, circuits, and magnetic effects of electric currents.

    • Electric fields and potential
    • Heating effect of electric currents
    • Electric cells and circuits
    • Magnetic effects of electric currents
    • Electromagnetic induction

  • Topic 6: Circular Motion and Gravitation 5 hours

    Advanced concepts in circular motion and gravitational fields.

    • Circular motion dynamics
    • Newton's law of universal gravitation
    • Gravitational fields and potential
    • Orbital motion and satellite mechanics
    • Escape velocity concepts

  • Topic 7: Atomic, Nuclear and Particle Physics 14 hours

    Structure of matter from atoms to fundamental particles.

    • Discrete energy and radioactivity
    • Nuclear reactions and energy
    • The structure of matter
    • Radioactive decay processes
    • Elementary particles and fundamental interactions

  • Internal Assessment Preparation

    Comprehensive support for your individual investigation project.

    • Research question development and experimental design
    • Data collection techniques and safety considerations
    • Statistical analysis and uncertainty evaluation
    • Evaluation of methodology and sources of error
    • Report writing and presentation skills
