Course Overview

Our IB Maths AA SL course is designed for students with strong analytical skills aiming to build a solid foundation in calculus, algebra, and mathematical proof. This track emphasizes logical reasoning, abstraction, and problem-solving — ideal for students interested in engineering, physics, or economics.

IB Assessment Information

External Assessment (80%): Paper 1 (No calculator - 90 min), Paper 2 (Calculator - 90 min)

Internal Assessment (20%): Mathematical Exploration - a written report on a chosen topic (6–12 pages)

Internal Assessment (IA) Support

Guidance for selecting a strong topic, framing mathematical questions, developing logical structure, refining arguments, and using effective mathematical language. We ensure clarity, creativity, and rigor.

What You'll Learn

  • Master algebraic manipulation, functions, and trigonometry
  • Understand calculus fundamentals: limits, derivatives, integrals
  • Develop proof and reasoning skills across topics
  • Apply mathematics to real-world modeling problems
  • Craft a strong Internal Assessment with depth and originality

Course Curriculum

  • Topic 1: Number & Algebra 18 hours

    • Arithmetic, radicals, logarithms
    • Exponents, sequences and series
    • Binomial expansion and mathematical induction

  • Topic 2: Functions 17 hours

    • Function notation, transformations
    • Inverse, composite functions
    • Polynomials, rational functions

  • Topic 3: Geometry & Trigonometry 17 hours

    • Trigonometric ratios and identities
    • Equations and applications in modeling
    • 2D vectors and applications

  • Topic 4: Statistics & Probability 16 hours

    • Descriptive statistics and distributions
    • Probability rules and expected value
    • Normal and binomial distributions

  • Topic 5: Calculus 12 hours

    • Limits and continuity
    • Differentiation and applications
    • Integration and area under curves

  • Internal Assessment Support

    • Topic brainstorming and feasibility check
    • Structure, mathematical depth, and clarity
    • Editing and citation advice
