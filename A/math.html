Course Overview

Our A Level Mathematics course provides students with a comprehensive foundation in pure mathematics, statistics, and mechanics. This rigorous two-year program develops advanced problem-solving skills, mathematical reasoning, and analytical thinking essential for success in STEM fields, economics, and many other university disciplines.

A Level Mathematics is one of the most respected and sought-after qualifications, opening doors to prestigious university courses including engineering, mathematics, physics, economics, and medicine. Our program ensures students develop both computational fluency and deep conceptual understanding, preparing them for the mathematical demands of higher education and professional careers.

Course Curriculum

  • Algebra and Functions AS Year 25 hours

    Advanced algebraic techniques, polynomial functions, and rational functions.

  • Coordinate Geometry AS Year 18 hours

    Straight lines, circles, and coordinate-based problem solving.

  • Sequences and Series AS Year 12 hours

    Arithmetic and geometric progressions, binomial expansion.

  • Trigonometry AS Year 20 hours

    Trigonometric functions, identities, and equations.

  • Exponentials and Logarithms AS Year 15 hours

    Exponential and logarithmic functions and their applications.

  • Differentiation AS Year 20 hours

    Calculus fundamentals, derivatives, and applications to optimization.

  • Integration AS Year 18 hours

    Basic integration techniques and applications to area calculations.

  • Advanced Calculus A2 Year 25 hours

    Advanced differentiation and integration techniques, differential equations.

  • Numerical Methods A2 Year 12 hours

    Iterative methods, numerical integration, and root finding.

  • Vectors A2 Year 15 hours

    Vector operations, lines, and planes in 2D and 3D space.

  • Statistics Both Years 20 hours

    Data analysis, probability distributions, and hypothesis testing.

  • Mechanics Both Years 20 hours

    Forces, motion, projectiles, and mathematical modeling of physical situations.
