Course Overview

Our A Level Physics course is designed to provide students with a deep understanding of fundamental physics principles and their applications. This comprehensive two-year program covers all aspects of the A Level Physics syllabus, from classical mechanics to modern physics, with emphasis on mathematical problem-solving, practical skills, and exam technique.

A Level Physics is essential for students planning to study physics, engineering, medicine, or any STEM field at university. The course develops critical thinking, mathematical skills, and scientific understanding that are highly valued by top universities and employers. Our program ensures students not only achieve excellent grades but also develop the analytical mindset needed for success in higher education.

Course Curriculum

  • Measurements and Uncertainties AS Year 8 hours

    Foundation skills in scientific measurement, data analysis, and uncertainty evaluation.

  • Mechanics AS Year 22 hours

    Motion, forces, work, energy, momentum, and circular motion.

  • Electric Circuits AS Year 16 hours

    Current, voltage, resistance, and electrical power in DC circuits.

  • Materials AS Year 12 hours

    Properties of materials, stress, strain, and elastic behavior.

  • Waves and Particle Nature of Light AS Year 14 hours

    Wave properties, interference, diffraction, and photoelectric effect.

  • Further Mechanics A2 Year 10 hours

    Projectile motion, uniform circular motion, and simple harmonic motion.

  • Thermal Physics A2 Year 12 hours

    Temperature, heat, internal energy, and gas laws.

  • Fields and Their Consequences A2 Year 18 hours

    Gravitational, electric, and magnetic fields with their applications.

  • Nuclear Physics A2 Year 14 hours

    Atomic structure, radioactivity, nuclear reactions, and applications.

  • Optional Topics A2 Year 14 hours

    Choice of astrophysics, medical physics, engineering physics, or electronics.
